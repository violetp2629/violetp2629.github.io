Freedom Project

The “Freedom Project” for SEP10 is all about applying technology to real-world topics. The purpose of this project is to form ideas about how technology will change in the future, and what new adaptations and/or creations can help to improve it. My partner, Valentine Montana, collaborated with me in order to create The Future Of Visual Arts, a website meant to briefly teach others about the history of art innovations. It was made using Tailwind CSS, as well as a few Bootstrap components.

Like any ongoing project, we encountered plenty of hardships while working. For one, we wanted our website to look a certain way when on certain sized screens (two columns when big, single column when small). In theory, we thought this would be easy since we had our wireframe to take reference from. It was not. I ended up complicating the process due to thinking I needed to justify/align the positioning, rather than just use <column> classes. Eventually, after many hours of testing and troubleshooting, we were able to figure it out, and our website responded accordingly.
